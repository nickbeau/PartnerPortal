Troubleshooting Surface Pro 4 issues

If you have purchased a Surface Pro 4 it is possible that you may be experiencing some unexpected behaviour.

 

The most common problem we see with the Surface Pro 4 is encountered when unplugging the device from a docking station.

 

Docking Station Issues

 

Ideally you should be able to remove the cable that connects the Surface to the docking station and the Surface should instantly come alive and be interactive.

 

Some Surface Pro 4 owners are finding that if they do remove the device from the docking station the Surface screen does not activate and the device cannot be interacted with forcing the individual to perform a soft reboot.

 

Soft Reboot procedure:

 

•Press and hold the power button on your Surface for 30 seconds.

•The screen may flash, but continue to hold down the power button for the full 30 seconds. After the full 30 seconds, release the power button.

 

WORKAROUND:

 

To avoid the above scenario shut-down the Surface before disconnecting the device from the docking station.

 

We expect that the docking station issue will be resolved in a future update from Microsoft.

 

 

 

Blue screen crashes and random lockups

 

A blue screen error (also called a stop error or Blue Screen of Death(BSOD)) can occur if a problem causes your PC to shut down or restart unexpectedly. When you experience this type of error, you won’t be able to see things like the Start menu or the taskbar on the screen when your PC is turned on. Instead you might see a blue screen with a message that your PC ran into a problem and needs to restart.

 

Diagnosing the BSOD's and lockups can be tricky but you can take steps to prevent them from reoccurring.

 

First thing to do is to ensure that your Surface's operating system, drivers and firmware is completely up to date and fully patched.

 

To do this:

 

1.Go to Start Windows logo , and select Settings  > Update & security > Windows Update.

2.Select Check for updates. If updates are available, they will install automatically.

You may need to restart your Surface after the updates have installed.

 

The Windows Update process will deliver updates for the Operating System, the firmware and the device drivers for the Surface.

 

Applying the most recent updates will resolve all sort of problems and is the most important thing you can do to keep your device up and running smoothly.

 

Another cause of the BSODs can be newly added hardware e.g accessories connected by USB

 

If you have added a device to your Surface and subsequently the Operating System has crashed remove the device and seek further IT assistance.
