Keep info secure while being productive

 

 

 

 

 

                                                                                                   Keep information secure while being productive

Back at the office, Karen wants to finish reviewing her email so she opens Outlook 2016 by typing Outlook in the Search bar on her desktop.

 

 

 

First, Karen reviews her Clutter folder to view emails marked as unimportant to help train Clutter to capture emails correctly. She navigates to the left menu and clicks Clutter. Clutter uses the power of Office Graph to learn from your actions what messages you are likely to ignore. Over time, less important messages will be moved to the Clutter folder, helping emails that interest you most float to the top of your inbox.

 

 

Back in her inbox, Karen clicks on the email titled Contoso’s Annual Meeting – Happy Hour! from Vice President of Human Resources Amy Alberts.

 

 

Karen isn't familiar with where the happy hour is being held, so she clicks on Bing Maps to quickly view the event's location. Now, she won't have to worry about how to get to the venue because she has easy access to directions.

 

 

She then clicks on Action Items, which suggests creating an action item to ensure Karen has RSVP'd by the end of the week. She clicks on Follow up and the action is automatically flagged so that Karen won't forget.

 

 
Next, Karen remembers she needs to email her boss, Ty Carlson, about a disciplinary issue with an employee. Not wanting the email to fall into the wrong hands, she types a quick email to Ty and adds confidential to the subject line to ensure the email cannot be forwarded. She then clicks Send.

 

 

 

 

Next, Karen needs to send her new credit card number to her travel agency for an upcoming convention. She starts an email to BobH@proseware.com and clicks Check Names.

 

 

In the body of the email, she types: 

 

Bob, 

 

For the upcoming conference, please use my updated credit card to reserve my room.  

 

Visa CC# 4111-1111-1111-1111 

Expires: 08/2018 

 

Karen 

 

As she types the message, she sees a Policy Tip appear at the top of the email telling her that the message cannot be sent due to the sensitive information it contains. 

 

 

Karen determines she’ll have to call her contact directly to comply with Contoso's security policies, so she clicks Discard on her draft.

 

Lastly, Karen sees the email from Carlos that she flagged from her phone this morning.

 

 

She decides to set up a meeting to brief the Sales and Operations teams later that afternoon, so she replies to Carlos with a Skype for Business meeting by clicking on Meeting in the Respond section of the ribbon.

 

 

 

 

Then, from within the meeting invite, she clicks Skype meeting.

 

 

Facilitation Tip: Now, PSTN Conferencing is included in Skype for Business which saves companies money. 

 

Next, she wants to add Meeting Notes. Karen will add her Notebook she used while talking with the store manager. From the meeting invite, she clicks Meeting Notes.

 

 

Next, Karen clicks Share notes with the meeting.

 

 

Karen will see a list of all her shared Notebooks. She selects Store Visits and clicks OK.

 

 

With her Notebook added to the Meeting invite, Karen would also like to add the PowerPoint presentation she started working on earlier. Karen can attach the PowerPoint from her OneDrive account directly from Outlook utilizing Modern Attachments. Karen clicks Insert > Attach File from the ribbon.

 

 

Here, she will see a list of her recent files, as well Browse Web Locations and Browse This PC. She selects Browse Web Locations and selects OneDrive.

 

 

Next, Karen selects the PowerPoint presentation she created earlier, and clicks Insert.

 

 

With Modern Attachments, she can attach the file as a copy from her OneDrive account and change permissions to view or edit. Karen leaves the permission to "Recipients can edit."

 

 

She adds the Operations and Business Strategy and Sales distribution lists to the To: field, adds a quick message to add context for the meeting, schedules a date and time, then clicks Send.

 

 

 

As Karen finishes her email review, a colleague stops by her office in panic, telling her that he left his Windows Phone on the city bus. He's concerned the confidential work files he was reviewing on his phone are in jeopardy. Karen calms him down by reminding him to contact Contoso's IT department who can help him conduct a remote wipe of his device.

 

They can conduct a Selective Wipe of his Windows Phone, which would remove all IT provisioned settings and stored corporate data, without even touching his personal settings and data.

 

With the crisis averted, Karen can begin researching the M400 presale issue.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Facilitation Tip:

Internet connectivity is required for Bing Maps and Action Item apps to function as intended. Also, the Outlook account must be connected online for these services to work. If you receive an error in Bing maps, click on File > Office Account. Your persona name should be listed in Connected Services. If it is not, click Go online to connect the account to the tenant.

Facilitation Tip:

This is also a good time to discuss the benefits of Selective Wipe and Workplace Join on PCs. With Workplace Join, users can remotely register a non-Domain Joined device in Active Directory (AD) to gain secure “single sign-on” access to permitted corporate network resources from a BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) PC. For example, if Karen were visiting a reseller's office, the store could give her device access to their network so she could review company documents. When she leaves the reseller's office, the reseller can perform a Selective Wipe on her device to remove any documents she had access to while in her meeting.  Selective Wipe removes IT provisioned settings and stored corporate data from the device permanently without touching the user’s personal settings and data.
