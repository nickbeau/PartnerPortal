Mobile Device & App Management - WinPhone

 

Karen starts her day on her corporate Windows Phone and enters her password that is required by company security policies implemented by Contoso IT.

Facilitation Tip:

This section requires additional set up steps to complete. Please refer to the Technical Guide found on the CIE portal for details

 

Facilitation Tip: This section is written for the Windows Phone 8.1. If you have an iOS device, you can use the following section Mobile Device & App Management iPhone

 

Next, Karen wants to take a quick photo of a competitor's advertisement she received in the mail so she can send it to the advertising department. She opens the camera on her phone and discovers the video and camera have been disabled. Contoso is announcing a new device this week and IT wants to prevent any unauthorized videos of the new product from being leaked.

She also notices social media apps that are blocked on her phone for the same reason. Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram all have been temporarily disabled on her phone to prevent accidental posting about the new device as the final public release preparations are made.

 
Once the announcement is made and the embargo is over, IT will re-enable these features.

If you would like to experience the ease of re-enabling these features, navigate to the Intune Admin Console.

 
Click on Policy > Configuration Policies > Win phone 8.1 and up policy > Edit to modify the policies for enrolled Windows Phone devices.

In the Device Capability group, the IT Admin can change Allow Camera from No to Yes to re-enable the cameras on enrolled devices.

Facilitation Tip: It will take a few minutes for the policy change to sync with enrolled phones, so time this step so you can talk about the benefits of Mobile Device Management and Mobile App Management capabilities of the EMS Suite. You can also open the Company Store and sync the device again to force the policy change and re-enable the features.

 

In the Allowed & Blocked Apps list, the IT Admin can delete any of the listed apps to make them available again on enrolled devices.
