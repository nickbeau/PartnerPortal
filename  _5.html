Coauthor and share content quickly

 

Karen wants her team to help her coauthor M400 marketing messages in PowerPoint 2016, so she navigates to the Contoso intranet and clicks Sales & Marketing > Collateral Center.

 

 

 

She clicks Edit Presentation > Edit in PowerPoint on the M400 Promotion PowerPoint presentation.

 

 

 

She clicks on the Share icon in the ribbon to invite Alan Steiner, Kelly Krout and her other teammates.

 

 

Karen clicks the names of her coworkers in the Share window and clicks Share.

 

 

They all receive an email with the PowerPoint presentation to open and coauthor.

 

 

While working in separate browsers, Karen edits slides 3 at the same time Kelly is making changes to slide 4.

 

 

 

 

At the same time, Alan adds a slide to the deck by right-clicking and selecting New Slide.
 

 

 

He needs to add an image of a smartphone to this slide, but doesn't have one handy. He navigates to the second slide, right-clicks on the word "smartphone" and clicks Smart Lookup.

 

 

With Smart Lookup, Office 2016 searches the Internet for images and content to make completing your task easier.

 

Alan finds a picture he wants to use in his slide. He clicks on the picture and he is taken to the image in his browser. He saves the image to his desktop.

 

 

Now, he wants to add the picture into his blank slide. To quickly insert the picture, he clicks on the Tell me what you want to do… bar in the ribbon and starts typing Insert Pictures.

 

 

He clicks the Insert Pictures option from the menu and selects the image he saved to his desktop earlier. PowerPoint adds the picture to the slide. He is satisfied with his update and clicks the Save icon to save the changes to SharePoint.

 

 

Now, Alan's teammates can see his changes in the most up-to-date presentation.
