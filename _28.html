Mobile Device & App Management iPhone

 

Karen starts her day on her corporate iPhone and enters her password that is required by company security policies implemented by Contoso IT.

Next, Karen wants to take a quick photo of a competitor's advertisement she received in the mail so she can send it to the advertising department. She scans her phone for the camera app and discovers the camera and video camera have been disabled. She remembers that Contoso is announcing a new device this week and IT wants to prevent any unauthorized videos of the new product from being leaked.

Once the announcement is made and the embargo is over, IT will re-enable these features. (Note: See step below if you would like to re-enable this feature as part of the CIE session.)

 

Karen also notices the Apple Store app is not available as part of IT's management of corporate iPhones so employees can't add apps to their enrolled phones without permission from IT.

If you would like to experience the ease of re-enabling these features, navigate to the Intune Admin Console.

 
Click on Policy > Configuration Policies > iOS 7.1 and up policy > Edit to modify the policies for enrolled iPhone devices.

In the Device Capability group, you can change Allow Camera from No to Yes to re-enable the cameras on enrolled devices.

 
Facilitation Tip: It takes a few minutes for the policy change to sync with enrolled phones, so time this step so you can talk about the benefits of Mobile Device Management and Mobile App Management capabilities of the EMS Suite. You can also open the Company Store and sync the device again to force the policy change and re-enable the features.

 To re-enable the Apple store, navigate to the Apps group and change Allow application store to Yes.
