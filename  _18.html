Information Protection

 

As the VP of Operations and Business Strategy, Karen Berg is finalizing a company internal communication regarding a restructuring of the Operations Department. This reorganization will be announced at the end of the month and she would like to get input on the confidential draft she has created from the VP of Human Resources, Amy Alberts. Thanks to Microsoft Azure Rights Management Service, Karen can protect the sensitive information and control access to the document, even when it is shared with others.

 

Karen wants to share the Departmental Reorganization document she has saved in her OneDrive with Amy. She types Word 2016 in the search bar to find and open Word.

 

She clicks on Open Other Documents…

 

then clicks on OneDrive - Contoso > Departmental Reorganization.docx.

 

Karen clicks on Share Protected > Share Protected…

 

and signs in to the Microsoft Right Management App.

 

 

She adds amya@<tenantname>.onmicrosoft.com to the users box and sets her permission level to Viewer - View Only so she can only view and not print, edit, or copy the content in the document.

 

 

She also checks the box next to Expire these documents on and enters a date for the expiration date, chooses the option to be notified when Amy accesses the document, and checks Allow me to instantly revoke.

 

Karen clicks send and a draft email opens in Outlook with Amy's email address populated and the protected document attached. Karen writes an intro so Amy understands what she needs and clicks Send.

 

 

Amy Alberts is signed into Outlook and reviews the document in her preview window. She also wants view the document in Word, so she double-clicks on the attachment and is required to sign in to open the document.

 

 

Each time Amy opens the document, Karen receives a notification.

 

 

 

Amy replies to Karen's email and tells her that she thinks the document looks good.

 

Amy thinks Karen has done a great job with communication regarding the departmental reorganization and she wants to let Karen's boss know about Karen's good work. She clicks on Karen's email and clicks Forward and writes a note of recognition for Karen's hard work.

 

In the To line, she includes Ty's work address and his personal email address, because she can't remember whether or not Ty is on vacation.

 

Facilitation note: To demonstrate the security of the protected document, use another session participant's Contoso email address (TyC@tenant.onmicrosoft.com) as well as someone's external corporate email address to forward the original email. Please note that generic email domains such as @hotmail or @gmail accounts will be blocked and private email domains will receive the email but the recipient will not be able to open the document.

 

After Karen receives the email from Amy, she knows that Amy is finished reviewing the document, so she can revoke Amy's access to the document.

 

In the ribbon of the open Word document, she clicks on Share Protected > Track Usage and Sign in, which opens the RMS Document Tracking Center.

 

Here, Karen clicks on the Departmental Reorganization document where she can review statistics on the document and also revoke access to the document by clicking on Revoke > Confirm.

 

 

 

Microsoft Rights Management services (RMS) help organizations keep their information secure both inside and outside their boundaries, by protecting documents both at rest and in motion. Type of file, location, and device-independent, RMS offers seamless collaboration across organizations and more.

 

 

Facilitation Tip: Karen Berg's Outlook 2016 should remain open during this section so email invitations can be sent to Amy Alberts.
